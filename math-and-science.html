Math and Science

Bachelor of Science in Mathematics

Bachelor's degree programs in mathematics cover the essential areas of calculus, geometry, algebra and statistics. Students in this degree program can choose a general mathematics track, which prepares them for teaching and graduate study. Alternatively, they may choose an applied mathematics track - mathematical concepts are applied in various scientific disciplines, such as engineering and the natural sciences. Other concentration areas might include statistics, computer science and economics. Mathematics degree programs are available in conjunction with teacher education programs that allow graduates to teach mathematics in elementary or secondary schools.

Coursework in this degree program varies from school to school and is dependent on the area of concentration. Teacher certification students take teaching and curriculum courses in addition to mathematics requirements. Common courses in this degree program include:

  • Discrete structures
  • Applied analysis
  • Differential structures
  • Advance calculus
  • Linear algebra

Bachelor of Science in Biology

Biology is a scientific area of study where students learn about the biological processes of human, plant and animal life. Students can choose from several concentration areas, including ecology and microbiology as well as preparatory courses for premedical and predental programs. Students take courses in chemistry, physics and general biology in the early stages of this degree program and later move on to major courses, such as cellular biology, genetics and laboratory studies. Students may elect to minor in marine biology or geology or pursue teacher certification at the elementary or secondary level.

Biology students take fundamental biology courses in combination with laboratory coursework. Students might perform dissections or study microorganisms and cells. Common courses in this degree program include:

  • Marine sciences
  • Immunology
  • Anatomy
  • Molecular biology
  • Microbiology

Bachelor of Arts in Chemistry

Bachelor of Arts degree programs in chemistry are intended for students who plan to teach, pursue graduate studies or enter a professional career, such as a doctor, dentist or veterinarian. Students can choose from several concentrations in forensic science, biochemistry, environmental chemistry or engineering. Students take fundamental chemistry courses, such as organic chemistry, physical chemistry and biochemistry and complete laboratory courses as part of their degree requirements. Undergraduate chemistry degree programs are also offered in partnership with teacher certification programs, which prepare students to teach chemistry in secondary schools.

Coursework in this degree program includes the study of biology, physics and calculus. Many courses have a laboratory component. Other courses might include:

  • Analytical chemistry
  • Thermodynamics
  • Quantitative analysis
  • Statistical mechanics
  • Polymers
